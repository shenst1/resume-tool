# SWOT — Tenna (Senior Front-end Engineer)

## Strengths

- **Clear product domain:** construction equipment and operations—tangible user stories (assets, maintenance, utilization) for a frontend engineer to latch onto.
- **Senior scope** in the posting: ownership of quality, documentation, evaluations of tools, and agile participation—not a “pixel pusher only” narrow role.
- **Remote-friendly** with explicit benefits (401k match, PTO, etc.).
- **Strategic backing:** under **John Deere** ownership (per Deere newsroom, 2025–2026), which can mean stability and capital for product investment—also verify how autonomous Tenna’s product/engineering culture remains.

## Weaknesses

- **Travel 8–10%** may be a constraint if you optimize for zero travel.
- **Polyglot encouragement** (C#/.NET/Python) hints some legacy or adjacent services; you may touch boundaries outside pure React—even if your daily work is JS/TS.
- **AWS depth** in the post (Elasticsearch, Kinesis, VPC) may be more **platform-team** than day-one frontend; worth clarifying how much **frontend engineers** are expected to own vs consume.

## Opportunities

- **Design system and workflow UX:** construction software often has room to modernize dense tables, maps, and multi-step flows—high leverage for a senior frontend hire.
- **Data-informed iteration:** post mentions user behavior and monitoring—good fit if you like closing the loop with product and support signals.
- **Post-acquisition roadmap:** potential for tighter **equipment OEM** integrations and enterprise expectations on reliability and compliance UX.

## Threats

- **Post-merger integration** can shift roadmaps, tooling, or reporting lines; ask how engineering priorities are set today.
- **Niche domain** means interviewers may probe **construction or fleet** empathy—prepare examples of **operator-grade** or **field-adjacent** software even if not construction-specific.

## Notes

- **Verify:** Team topology (web vs mobile ownership), on-call expectations, and which **AWS** surfaces frontend engineers touch in practice.
- **Education:** role lists CS degree or equivalent—in **interview**, be ready with a one-liner on **longevity, scope, and leadership-through-IC** alongside formal degree.
