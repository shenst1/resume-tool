## Reflection — feedback on assumptions

**“The door is closed.”**  
Treat as **unknown**, not fact. Many companies allow **reapplication after a cooldown** or different reqs; a single screen rarely tattoos you for life. If Builder mattered long-term, you could revisit **after** deliberate prep or a different referral path — not urgent, just don’t globalize one round into permanent identity.

**“Should have cancelled.”**  
The lesson is **consent and timing**: you’re allowed to **not** take every screen. A polite “I’m not in a position to represent my best on timed algorithms right now; I’ll pass / circle back” is often **less damaging** than a train wreck — *if* you can stomach walking away from that lottery ticket.

**“Cheating”**  
In-interview dishonesty usually **isn’t** worth it: reputational risk, imposter stress, and inconsistent signal if you land the job. What *does* work: **drill the pattern library** (trees, graphs, topo for dependencies), **timed practice**, **pair sessions**, **allowed** references where the platform permits. That’s not cheating — it’s **stacking the deck before you sit down**.

**The actual skill gap here**  
“Traverse tree + dependency check” maps to standard **graph/tree** tooling: DFS/BFS, **cycle detection**, **topological sort** on a DAG for dependency order. That’s **learnable in a focused block** when/if you decide this funnel is worth it — not a comment on your engineering for product work.
