# SWOT — Groundfloor (Senior Software Engineer)

## Strengths

- **Full-stack depth:** TypeScript, React/Next.js, **production React Native (Ply)**, Ruby on Rails in production, and PostgreSQL align with core parts of the stack and posting’s “expertise in one or more” framing.
- **Fintech and money movement:** Stripe, Tilia payments context, and a **B.S. in Finance** map well to an investment platform and regulated-adjacent thinking.
- **Integrations muscle:** Bloom-era third-party APIs and Plantfolio email/onboarding mirror “marketing and analytics platforms” and funnel work, even without Segment on your résumé today.
- **Collaboration:** Repeated cross-functional work with product and design; informal mentorship through pairing and reviews.

## Weaknesses

- **Vue.js:** Not your recent daily stack—be ready to discuss **transferable patterns** from React and a concrete ramp plan.
- **CDP / attribution:** Segment and deep-linking specifics may need on-the-job depth; you can speak to **event and integration patterns** generically.

## Opportunities

- **Growth pod:** You can anchor **mobile (RN)** with production history while ramping **Vue** on web—or they may emphasize **Rails + Postgres** depth for experiments and integrations.
- **Alternative investing narrative:** Your finance background is an easy story for **investor-facing** features and **trust** in the product.

## Threats

- **Stack literalism:** Some hiring managers filter for Vue keywords. Counter with **shipping record**, **Ruby + Postgres**, **RN production**, and **Vue ramp** in the first screen.

## Notes

- Ask how **surface ownership** is divided (one engineer across all three vs specialization) and what **experimentation stack** (A/B tooling) they use today.
- Confirm **comp band** and **equity** early if not in the posting.
