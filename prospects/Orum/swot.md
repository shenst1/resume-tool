# SWOT — Orum (Senior Full Stack Software Engineer, Coaching + Analytics)

## Strengths

- Clear product wedge: **live conversations** and **coaching** on top of dialer scale, not a generic CRM add-on.
- **Remote-first** org with a defined **full-stack** lane and backend-heavy balance fits your production center of gravity.
- Stack overlap on **TypeScript**, **Node.js**, **React**, **PostgreSQL**, and **Ruby** reduces ramp on the majority of application code.
- **High-volume real-time** domain is differentiated; strong engineers who learn telephony can compound.

## Weaknesses

- **Telephony stack** (FreeSWITCH, SIP, audio, speech) is a steep specialty; your résumé has no tenure there.
- **Sales/GTM domain** is new vocabulary; ramp on user mental models and compliance-adjacent workflows.
- **GCP** is required platform depth; your strongest cloud miles are **AWS** today.
- Competitive dialer/coaching market (Nooks, Koncert, legacy SEP dialers, Gong-adjacent coaching).

## Opportunities

- **Coaching + Analytics** team turns existing call firehose into sticky product beyond dialer commodity.
- **AI on proprietary call corpus** (billions of calls claimed) is a moat if insight quality ships faster than wrappers.
- **Integrations-first** posture (CRM/SEP) matches your integration and API discipline from Bloom and agency work.
- Senior IC with **mentorship** expectations aligns with how you already lead (reviews, pairing, no direct reports).

## Threats

- Dialer/connect-rate features can become table stakes; coaching must stay defensible.
- Real-time outages or spam/reputation issues hit revenue teams immediately (high operational bar).
- Funding environment may pressure efficiency after 2022 Series B (verify current runway in interview).
- Candidate pool may include engineers with direct **VoIP/telephony** backgrounds.

## Notes

- Clarify **on-call** expectations for real-time audio vs web app tiers.
- Verify **US remote** states, comp band, and whether posting is **team-specific** or general full-stack pool.
