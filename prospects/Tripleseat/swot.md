# SWOT — Tripleseat (Principal Engineer, UI platform)

## Strengths

- **Clear vertical focus:** Deep workflow software for **venues and event sales**—buyers feel pain acutely (calendar chaos, contracts, deposits), which supports retention when the product works.
- **Scale signals (posting):** JD cites **thousands of locations**, high event volume, and large **event revenue** through the platform—suggesting real dependency on the product if accurate.
- **AI story with UI leverage:** Investing in a **single modern component stack** plus **AI-native workflows** can compound: faster shipping externally and internally if executed with discipline.

## Weaknesses

- **Migration risk:** **Kendo → shadcn** while **collapsing two front-ends** is high coordination debt; easy to underestimate edge cases in **older modules** and **customer-specific behaviors**.
- **Two-sided complexity:** Venues, brands, and payment/compliance variants can create **long tail UI** that strains a design system—needs explicit governance.
- **Hospitality cyclicality:** Macro events (corporate travel, dining spend) can pressure customers’ budgets—roadmaps may pivot.

## Opportunities

- **AI features that earn trust:** Thoughtful **human-in-the-loop**, **transparent tool calls**, and **streaming UX** can differentiate from “chat bolted onto forms.”
- **Internal leverage:** **Cursor/Claude Code** plus a possible **UI MCP** could shorten migration and tighten consistency—if standards are enforced, not optional.
- **Consolidated UX:** A unified stack is a chance to **raise accessibility and performance** org-wide in one motion.

## Threats

- **Competitive SaaS:** Adjacent event/hospitality tools and **horizontal CRMs** can encroach from above or below on specific workflows.
- **Talent market:** Principal IC bar is high; **execution risk** if the role becomes mostly meetings versus hands-on system ownership.
- **Tech churn:** Rapid AI tooling change can fragment teams without strong **conventions** and **examples**.

## Notes

- **Verify in interview:** Actual **roadmap** for React 19, **timeline** for Kendo retirement, **design system governance** model, and how **“UI MCP”** is scoped (internal-only vs. broader).
- **Metrics:** Prefer **official investor or press** numbers over third-party estimates when you cite scale externally.
