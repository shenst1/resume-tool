# SWOT — Finite State (Senior Full-Stack Software Engineer)

## Strengths

- **Stack match:** Posting centers on Next.js, TypeScript, PostgreSQL/Supabase, RLS, and AI-native workflows—direct overlap with Plantfolio and your daily practice.
- **Data-centric security:** Mongo production then Postgres on Neon; Supabase explored but not shipped—Postgres/Clerk access patterns in production; identity-aware work at Tilia.
- **AI velocity:** Cursor-first habits and production AI features (Plantfolio) align with how they want engineers to work.

## Weaknesses

- **Domain:** Finite State’s core story is **connected device / firmware / supply chain** security; your background is **web apps and commerce/payments**, not SBOM or binary analysis. Be upfront that domain depth is something you build with the team.

## Opportunities

- **Migration moment:** Mongo→Postgres and provider evaluation (Neon over Supabase) is a natural story for teams pushing logic into Postgres/RLS.
- **RLS and authz:** Strong RLS experience is explicitly called out in the job; few candidates can point to production policies the way you can.

## Threats

- **Bar for “senior”** in security-heavy companies may include **AppSec or platform security** depth; interviews may probe threat modeling, secure SDLC, or supply chain concepts—prepare narratives from secure coding, authz, and review practices rather than claiming firmware expertise you do not have.

## Notes

- Confirm **comp tier** for your location (US vs Canada) and **title/level** expectations in the first recruiter screen.
- Ask how **Edge Functions** vs **Next.js API routes** are divided today and what the **RDS migration** timeline looks like.
