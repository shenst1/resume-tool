# SWOT — Topstep (Senior Full Stack Engineer)

## Strengths

- Posting stack aligns directly: **TypeScript**, **React**, **Next.js**, **GraphQL**, **REST**, **PostgreSQL**.
- **Payments/purchasing** charter matches **Stripe** (Plantfolio) and payments/KYC-adjacent delivery (Tilia).
- **Event-driven** and async production habits (webhooks, Inngest, background jobs) map to distributed-systems language in the JD.
- **12+ years** and end-to-end ownership exceed the **5+** bar; finance degree supports trader money-flow context.
- **Remote-to-hybrid** flexibility may suit candidates outside Chicago better than strict office mandates.

## Weaknesses

- **SQL Server** is listed alongside Postgres; résumé depth is **Postgres/Prisma** only.
- Not a career **futures trading platform** or **exchange-latency** engineer; reliability story is product/payments scale, not order-book microstructure.
- Company also hires **.NET/Kubernetes** platform roles—this TS role may still interface with teams on different stacks.
- Public 2025–2026 commentary on **TopstepX outages** raises the bar for operational credibility in interviews.

## Opportunities

- Payments backbone is high leverage as Combine, funded accounts, and TopstepX roadmap grow (including **TFD** integration).
- Comp band (**$157k–$193k**) competitive for senior full stack with bonus upside.
- Prop/fintech intersection is distinctive career narrative if you want regulated, high-stakes product work.
- Engineering culture explicitly values **standards**, **observability**, and **safe scaling**—matches platform-minded IC positioning.

## Threats

- Candidates with **SQL Server** + **payments at trading/fintech scale** may screen ahead.
- Platform stability scrutiny may favor SRE-heavy backgrounds for “keep the lights on” narrative.
- Prop-trading industry volatility and reputation swings (Trustpilot, payout policy changes) are business risk, not engineering blockers, but worth understanding.
