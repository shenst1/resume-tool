# SWOT — Neo.Tax (Senior Full-Stack Engineer, Platform)

## Strengths

- **Pain is real:** R&D credits and **capitalization** are expensive and slow with traditional advisors—software that **ingests contemporaneous records** addresses a clear workflow problem.
- **Data moat:** Deep **integrations** across **engineering and finance** systems can create switching costs once historical evidence lives in the product.
- **AI narrative (if disciplined):** “Show the work” positioning—if the product stays **explainable**—helps with **trust** in a regulated-adjacent market.

## Weaknesses

- **Buyer cynicism:** Finance leaders have seen **black-box** vendors; proof requires **defensible methodology** and **clean exports** for auditors and Big Four relationships.
- **Integration fragility:** Partners change APIs; **payroll** and **accounting** quirks multiply with **SMB to enterprise** spread—Platform must absorb variance.
- **Complexity of tax law:** Product and engineering must track **rule changes** and **jurisdiction** nuance—ongoing compliance cost.

## Opportunities

- **Expand SKUs:** Adjacent **credits**, **cost studies**, or **ASC 606**-related workflows could reuse the same ingestion spine—if data model is flexible enough.
- **Employer/partner channels:** Payroll and accounting platforms as **distribution** or **embedded** flows—if partnerships mature.
- **Enterprise penetration:** Logo wins (named on site/marketing) can shorten sales cycles—**if** enterprise readiness (SSO, controls) keeps pace.

## Threats

- **Regulatory and IRS scrutiny:** Category behavior can shift **safe-harbor** assumptions; bad headlines hurt the whole space.
- **Incumbents:** Traditional firms and **Big Tech** payroll/HR suites could bundle **lighter** automation.
- **Macro and startup budgets:** **VC-backed** customers tightening spend slows expansion revenue—**verify** current ICP emphasis in interview.
