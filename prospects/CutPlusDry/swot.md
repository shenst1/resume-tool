# SWOT — Cut+Dry (Senior Software Engineer)

## Strengths

- **Vertical wedge:** Purpose-built for **foodservice distribution**—catalog complexity, cut sizes, case packs, and route/account realities that horizontal commerce suites handle poorly.
- **Integration story:** Public materials lean on **many ERP connections**; if execution is strong, that is a moat against “generic B2B marketplace” clones.
- **AI-native positioning:** **AI Order Desk** / automation narratives align with how buyers actually behave (voicemail, email, texts) and with your posting’s **LLM** opportunity.

## Weaknesses

- **Enterprise hair:** Deep **ERP** and legacy data quirks can slow roadmap; every large distributor is a partial snowflake.
- **Stack heterogeneity:** A long list of **mandatory** languages (Java, JS, PHP, Python, etc.) can mean **real polyglot maintenance**—onboarding cost for any new senior hire.
- **Market education:** Distributors vary widely in digital maturity; **change management** can dominate pure product wins.

## Opportunities

- **US food supply digitization** still has runway; distributors that modernize ordering can capture share from laggards.
- **Restaurant operator UX**—if the network effects grow (distributors ↔ operators ↔ suppliers), the platform becomes stickier.
- **AI features with guardrails:** Order capture, recommendations, and risk signals can compound **if** data quality and human review loops are treated seriously.

## Threats

- **Incumbent and adjacent competition:** Large distributors’ homegrown stacks, other vertical SaaS, and horizontal **payments/order** players nibbling at workflows.
- **Macro:** Restaurant and hospitality spend shifts hit NRR if customers consolidate vendors.
- **AI hype cycle:** Buyers may tire of **LLM** demos without measurable **P95 latency**, accuracy, and support cost outcomes.

## Notes

- **Verify in interview:** Primary **language ownership** by feature team, **on-call**, **data model** (MySQL vs other stores in practice), and how **customer-facing** exposure works (frequency, travel).
- **Metrics** on customer outcomes: prefer **customer-permitted** or **published** figures over third-party aggregators.
