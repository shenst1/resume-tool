# SWOT — Fullstack Developer contract (React/Node.js)

## Strengths

- **Stack is a direct match:** TypeScript, React, Node.js, PostgreSQL in daily production on Plantfolio.
- **Role shape** (own workflows end to end, APIs, schema, production fixes) mirrors founder and senior IC history.
- **12+ years** exceeds typical contract senior bars.
- **Flexible hours** may fit alongside Plantfolio or other work if contract terms allow.
- **Remote** default.

## Weaknesses

- **Company unknown** — cannot research culture, funding, or legitimacy pre-apply.
- **Comp claim** ($230K–$280K/yr at 10–40 hrs/week) needs verification; may be annualized FTE equivalent or recruiting bait.
- LinkedIn **Easy Apply** + high comp + vague employer = elevated **scam/fake job** risk.
- Contract terms (IP, exclusivity, benefits, termination) unknown.

## Opportunities

- If legitimate, premium contract rate for exact stack could be efficient income without full-time lock-in.
- Short interview process (if real) enables fast signal.
- Portfolio proof (Plantfolio) is compelling for React/Node/Postgres screen.

## Threats

- **Fraudulent listings** are common at this comp band with minimal company detail.
- Part-time contract may conflict with other commitments if exclusivity is required.
- No equity or benefits vs W2 roles in pipeline (GiveCampus, Nourish, etc.).

## Notes

- **Before investing time:** Get company name, website, hiring manager LinkedIn, and written rate/hour formula.
- Ask: W2 contract or 1099? Expected weekly hours? Overlap with existing product (Plantfolio)?
- If company is revealed, rename prospect folder and update `target.company` on resume file.
- Apply only through verified channels; never pay fees or share sensitive credentials upfront.
