# SWOT — Scribd (Software Engineer II, Frontend — Signup & Activation)

## Strengths

- **React, TypeScript, Next.js** match required stack directly; production depth well above 4+ years.
- **Rails-backed frontend** experience (Bloom GraphQL + Next admin) fits “comfortable in Rails product environment.”
- **Onboarding and activation** proof on Plantfolio (auth, subscriptions, Stripe, transactional lifecycle).
- **Monorepo, shared components, testing, accessibility** align with squad expectations.
- **AI fluency** (Cursor top 1%) matches explicit posting requirement.
- **Remote documentation habits** from founder and platform work.

## Weaknesses

- **Senior title / 12+ years** vs. SE II level may trigger overqualification or comp mismatch ($125k+ floor still OK if level bands flex).
- **Formal growth engineering** (company A/B platform, personalization systems) not headline on resume.
- Multi-brand funnel (Scribd, Everand, SlideShare) is new domain vocabulary.
- Occasional in-person requirement under Scribd Flex needs location confirmation.

## Opportunities

- Growth squad is high visibility; signup/activation work has clear business metrics.
- Rails + modern React migration is a long arc; frontend hires who partner well on full-stack features are valuable.
- Posting emphasizes mentorship and growth toward autonomy—good if you want to lean into growth craft without platform title.

## Threats

- Candidates with dedicated **growth engineering** titles at consumer subscription companies may screen ahead.
- Internal candidates or referrals on Growth org.
- Level band may cap equity/comp vs. other senior pipeline roles you are pursuing.

## Notes

- Ask which **experimentation stack** they use (feature flags, A/B vendor, analytics) and how frontend ships variants.
- Clarify whether **GraphQL or REST** powers signup flows today.
- Confirm **level flexibility** given experience (SE II vs. senior track on same squad).
- Verify **approved metro** for your primary residence.
