# Post-rejection routine

Use this after any outcome you experience as a rejection (or a “no” you need to metabolize). The goal is to capture signal, regulate your nervous system, and return to the pipeline without spiraling.

## Step 1 — Write this down (literally)

Use a notebook or a file under `prospects/<company>/close-the-loop.md`. Force structure:

- **What went well** — list **3 things minimum** (even if it feels forced).
- **What I’d improve next time** — **at most 2** focused items (prevents rumination).
- **What was outside my control** — scope, timing, other candidates, role definition, business constraints.

## Step 2 — Close the loop

Say out loud if you can:

> “This was one attempt in a long pipeline.”

Pause. Let it land. You are allowed to care about the outcome and still treat it as one data point.

## Step 3 — Do something physical

Pick one:

- Walk  
- Ride  
- Shower  

Movement helps complete the stress cycle; it is not optional self-care fluff.

## Where things live

- **Process (this file):** `prospects/process.md`
- **Per company:** `prospects/<company>/close-the-loop.md` — recap, lessons, and reflective notes for that opportunity  
- **Tailored resumes:** `prospects/<company>/resumes/` — PDFs or exports for that employer  
- **Living prep reminders:** `prospects/interview-reminders.md` — patterns you want to bring into the next interview

Update `interview-reminders.md` whenever you learn something durable from a loop or a debrief.
